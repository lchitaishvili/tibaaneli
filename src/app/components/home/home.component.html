About us

In the eastern part of Kakheti, Georgia's most celebrated wine region, lies the picturesque village of Tibaani—a land where winemaking is not just a craft but a way of life. It is here that Tibaaneli Winery was born, carrying the soul of an 8,000-year-old tradition forward into the modern world.

For generations, Georgia has been known as the cradle of wine, a place where ancient winemaking techniques have remained untouched by time. At Tibaaneli, we honor this legacy by preserving the art of traditional Qvevri winemaking while embracing the possibilities of modern innovation. Every bottle we create is not just a drink—it is a testament to history, craftsmanship, and the land that nurtures us.

Export Countries

Since our establishment in 2020, Tibaaneli Winery has successfully expanded its reach across multiple international markets, bringing the authentic taste of Georgian wine to wine enthusiasts worldwide. Our wines are now available in Poland, the Czech Republic, Turkey, Azerbaijan, China, Kazakhstan, and Turkmenistan .

Each export market represents a unique opportunity to share Georgia's rich winemaking heritage. We carefully select our export partners to ensure that our wines are presented with the same care and attention to detail that we put into crafting them. Our international presence continues to grow, as we work to introduce more wine lovers to the exceptional quality and character of Georgian wines.

Our Special Location in Mtskheta

Welcome to our charming wine tasting room in the historic city of Mtskheta, Georgia's ancient capital. Nestled in this UNESCO World Heritage site, our location offers a perfect blend of history and modern wine culture. Step inside to experience our carefully curated selection of wines, where each tasting tells a story of Georgian winemaking tradition. Our knowledgeable staff will guide you through our wine portfolio, sharing insights about our unique terroir and winemaking techniques. Whether you're a wine enthusiast or a curious visitor, join us for an unforgettable journey through Georgia's rich wine heritage.
